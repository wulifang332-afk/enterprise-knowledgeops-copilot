# Cross-border Data Handling Policy

## Purpose and Scope

This policy governs cross-border handling of personal data and confidential regulated data in APAC and EU operations.

The Data Protection Office owns this policy and coordinates with Legal and Compliance for regional interpretation.

## Restricted Data Categories

Customer personal data, employee personal data, government identity numbers, payment details, health information, and confidential regulated business records are restricted data categories.

Restricted data must not be transferred to an unapproved external SaaS platform or unapproved overseas recipient.

## Approval Process

Cross-border transfer of personal data requires Data Protection Officer approval and an approved transfer mechanism before the transfer occurs.

The request must describe the data category, origin region, destination region, vendor or system, business purpose, retention period, and security controls.

## External SaaS Restrictions

Employees must not upload restricted data categories to unapproved external SaaS tools. Public AI tools are treated as unapproved external SaaS unless specifically listed in the approved software catalog.

If a business process requires external SaaS handling, the Data Protection Office must approve the system and transfer mechanism before use.

## Exceptions and Evidence

Exceptions require written Data Protection Office approval and Legal review when EU personal data is involved.

The request owner must retain approval evidence, transfer mechanism evidence, and vendor security evidence for audit review.
