# Customer Complaint Escalation SOP

## Purpose and Scope

This SOP defines how customer complaints are classified, escalated, and tracked. It applies to complaints received through support tickets, account teams, social channels, and executive escalations.

Customer Operations owns this SOP and coordinates with Regional Compliance for complaints involving regulatory, privacy, or conduct concerns.

## Complaint Classification

Severity 1 complaints involve potential regulatory breach, privacy impact, executive escalation, major customer harm, or repeated unresolved service failure.

Severity 2 complaints involve service failure for a single customer account without immediate regulatory impact. Severity 3 complaints are routine service issues.

## Severity and Escalation

A Severity 1 customer complaint must be escalated to the Customer Operations Director and Regional Compliance within 30 minutes of classification.

The complaint owner must record the classification reason, customer impact, and first response plan in the complaint management system.

## Response Ownership

Customer Support owns the first response. The Customer Operations Director owns Severity 1 coordination until the complaint is resolved or formally delegated.

Regional Compliance advises on regulatory notification, customer commitments, and required evidence retention.

## Closure and Evidence

Severity 1 complaints may be closed only after the customer response is sent, corrective actions are assigned, and Regional Compliance confirms that required evidence has been retained.

The complaint record must include the final customer response, root-cause summary, and action owner.
